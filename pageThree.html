Sik-Jang-San
'Sik Jang' Mountain

Sikjangsan Mountain is a mountain that spans Dong-gu, Daejeon, and Okcheon-gun, Chungcheongbuk-do. This mountain was also called Tanhyeon, and there is a legend that it was named after storing and fighting the military beauty of Baekje soldiers, as well as a legend that Jeon Woo-chi buried treasures that could be eaten for three years here in the past.[1] The water barrier dam at the entrance of the Secheon Amusement Park was built in 1934 by blocking the valley and was a drinking water source for Daejeon citizens until Daecheongho Lake was used as tap water, and it was the origin of Daejeoncheon Stream. There is a paved road from Secheon Amusement Park to the top, so you can go up by car. It's about four kilometers long. At the summit, there was a canteen vehicle in the past, selling coffee or simple food and beverage, but the pavilion was built in 2018 as it was maintained as a park. In December 2020, with the help of a local figure and Professor Jeong Tae-hee of the Department of Calligraphy Design at Daejeon University, a Korean signboard written "Sikjangru" was hung.
